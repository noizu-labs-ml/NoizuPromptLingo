# Market Intelligence Skill

**Identify, validate, and score underserved niches and audiences across any monetization stream.**

---

## Overview

The Market Intelligence skill provides the research and validation framework to identify profitable niches before you invest time and money building products. It works across all three income streams (AI Templates, Content Publishing, POD) to help you answer the critical first question: *"Is there actually a market for this?"*

**Core Purpose:**
- Discover underserved niches with passionate audiences
- Validate demand signals before building
- Profile target audiences (demographics, psychographics, pain points)
- Analyze competition and identify gaps
- Score opportunities to prioritize your efforts
- Understand audience size and willingness to pay

---

## When to Use This Skill

Use this skill:
- **Before building anything** - Validate your idea has a real market
- **To choose between ideas** - Score multiple opportunities and pick the highest-potential
- **To find your first niche** - Systematic discovery of underserved opportunities
- **To understand your audience** - Build detailed customer persona beyond assumptions

## Core Concepts

### What is a "Good" Niche?

A niche should have:

1. **Defined Audience**
   - Specific group of people (not "everyone interested in X")
   - Clear identity/community (reddit, Discord, forums)
   - Demonstrated passion/engagement

2. **Real Pain Points**
   - Specific problems they're frustrated about
   - Time or money at stake
   - Current solutions are inadequate

3. **Willingness to Pay**
   - Evidence they spend money on solutions (exist on Gumroad, similar products exist)
   - Income level supports your price point
   - Pain severe enough to justify investment

4. **Accessibility**
   - You can reach them (communities exist online)
   - Limited competition or clear gaps
   - You have insight/advantage in space

### Niche Scoring Framework

| Criterion | Weight | Scoring |
|-----------|--------|---------|
| **Market Size** | 15% | 1=tiny, 10=large and growing |
| **Pain Severity** | 20% | 1=nice-to-have, 10=critical daily frustration |
| **Willingness to Pay** | 20% | 1=no evidence, 10=proven buyers spending $100+ |
| **Competition Level** | 15% | 1=red ocean, 10=blue ocean/no direct competitors |
| **AI/Product Fit** | 15% | 1=poor fit, 10=perfectly suited to stream |
| **Your Advantage** | 15% | 1=no knowledge, 10=deep expertise/unique insight |

**Threshold:** Score 7.0+ = pursue; 5-7 = monitor; <5 = pass

## Research Process

### Phase 1: Discovery

**Goal:** Generate 10-20 potential niches to explore

**Sources:**
- Reddit: r/[industry], r/entrepreneur, niche subreddits
- Hacker News: Comments on relevant stories
- Twitter: #[niche] discussions, builds-in-public
- Facebook Groups: Industry-specific communities
- Indie Hackers: Problem discussions, market validation
- ProductHunt: Tool launches in your space
- YouTube: What are people searching for/struggling with?

**Questions to ask:**
- What problems does this community complain about?
- What solutions already exist?
- What gaps are there in current offerings?
- Would this community pay for a better solution?

### Phase 2: Initial Screening

**Goal:** Filter 10-20 ideas down to 5-7 serious contenders

**Quick Filter:**
- [ ] Audience size: 10K+ people interested
- [ ] Willingness to pay: Evidence of similar products sold
- [ ] AI fit: Problem suitable for templates, content, or design
- [ ] My advantage: I understand this space or can learn quickly
- [ ] Accessibility: I can reach this audience

**Pass if:** Audience too small, no proof of willingness to pay, or no competitive advantage

### Phase 3: Deep Dive Research

**Goal:** Thoroughly understand audience, pain points, competition

**For each promising niche:**

1. **Audience Profiling**
   - Demographics: Age, income, geography, education
   - Psychographics: Values, aspirations, identity
   - Professional context: Job titles, company sizes, industries
   - Online behavior: Platforms they use, communities they join

2. **Pain Point Mapping**
   - List 10+ specific problems this audience faces
   - Rank by severity and frequency
   - Identify which are underserved

3. **Competition Analysis**
   - Find 5-10 existing solutions
   - Note: Price, features, quality, customer satisfaction
   - Identify what's missing or done poorly
   - Find your competitive edge

4. **Willingness to Pay**
   - Find products already selling to this niche
   - What are they charging?
   - How are they monetized?
   - Evidence of customer satisfaction/repeat purchase?

### Phase 4: Validation

**Goal:** Confirm demand signals before building

**Validation Checklist:**
- [ ] Search volume exists (Google Trends shows interest)
- [ ] Community activity (subreddits/forums have recent posts)
- [ ] Existing solutions (competing products available)
- [ ] Price point validated (similar products sell at expected price)
- [ ] Customer pain confirmed (people explicitly asking for solutions)

### Phase 5: Scoring & Prioritization

**Goal:** Rank opportunities by opportunity score

Use the scoring rubric to calculate:
- Each dimension 1-10
- Multiply by weight
- Sum to get total score (max 10.0)

Example:
```
Market Size: 8 × 0.15 = 1.2
Pain Severity: 9 × 0.20 = 1.8
Willingness to Pay: 8 × 0.20 = 1.6
Competition: 7 × 0.15 = 1.05
AI Fit: 8 × 0.15 = 1.2
My Advantage: 7 × 0.15 = 1.05
TOTAL: 7.9 ✅ Pursue
```

## Audience Profiling Template

For your chosen niche, develop complete profile:

### Demographics
- Age range, gender distribution
- Income level, education
- Geographic concentration
- Professional background

### Psychographics
- Core values and identity
- Aspirations and goals
- Fears and frustrations
- How they see themselves

### Professional Context
- Job titles / roles
- Company sizes
- Industries
- Day-to-day challenges

### Online Behavior
- Platforms they use
- Communities (Reddit, Discord, Slack)
- Influencers they follow
- Content they consume

### Specific Pain Points
1. [Pain] - Severity: __/10 - How often: daily/weekly/monthly
2. [Pain] - Severity: __/10 - How often: ...
[etc - aim for 10+ specific pains]

### Purchasing Patterns
- How do they currently solve problems?
- How much do they spend annually?
- What's their buying process?
- Who influences purchases?

## Competitive Gap Analysis

### Direct Competitors (same format/price point)
- Analyze top 5 products
- What do customers love?
- What do they complain about?
- What's missing?

### Indirect Competitors (different format/price)
- Free resources (blogs, videos, communities)
- Courses ($50-500)
- Services (consulting, agencies)
- SaaS tools ($5-100/mo)

### Gaps & Opportunities
- Format gaps (no video course, no newsletter, no template pack)
- Quality gaps (existing products poorly made)
- Audience gaps (some segments underserved)
- Price gaps (too expensive or too cheap)

### Your Differentiation
- What can you do better?
- What unique insight do you have?
- How is your angle different?
- Why would customers choose you?

## Validation Signal Hierarchy

**Strong Signals (High Confidence):**
- ✅ Existing profitable products in space
- ✅ Large, active community discussing pain points
- ✅ High search volume for problem-related keywords
- ✅ People explicitly asking "how do I..."
- ✅ Customers complaining on social media

**Medium Signals (Moderate Confidence):**
- ⚠️ Growing interest (Google Trends upward)
- ⚠️ Some existing solutions
- ⚠️ Moderate community size
- ⚠️ Tangential evidence (related products popular)

**Weak Signals (Low Confidence):**
- ❌ Assumption ("I think people want...")
- ❌ Tiny communities
- ❌ No existing solutions (might be no demand)
- ❌ Declining search interest

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

| Mistake | Why It Fails | Prevention |
|---------|---|---|
| No audience size check | Too small to be profitable | Verify communities exist + size |
| Assuming vs. validating | Your assumptions wrong | Prove demand with research |
| Ignoring competition | Already solved well | Analyze what exists + gaps |
| Too broad niche | Too much competition | Get specific (not "productivity") |
| Underestimating effort | Niches harder than expected | Validate willingness to pay |
| No advantage | Anyone can build it | What makes YOUR version better? |
