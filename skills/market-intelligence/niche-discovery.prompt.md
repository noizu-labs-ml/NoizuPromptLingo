# Niche Discovery

Use this prompt to generate and discover underserved niche opportunities based on your skills, interests, and constraints.

---

## PROMPT: Generate Niche Ideas

This prompt helps you systematically explore viable niche opportunities using proven discovery frameworks. Generate 10-20 ideas, not just a handful—quantity leads to quality.

### What to Provide

Before using this prompt, gather:

```
Your Input:
- Skills: [List 5-10 skills/expertise areas you have]
- Interests: [List 5-10 topics you're passionate about]
- Constraints: [Time available/week, Budget, Technical limitations, etc.]
- Stream preference: [AI Templates | Content Publishing | POD | Multiple/flexible]
- Target timeline: [Quick wins (2-3 months) | Long-term build (6-12 months)]
```

### The Prompt

```markdown
I want to discover viable niche opportunities for [STREAM/MULTIPLE].

**My Profile:**
- Skills: [YOUR SKILLS]
- Interests: [YOUR INTERESTS]
- Constraints: [YOUR CONSTRAINTS]
- Timeline: [YOUR TIMELINE]

Generate 10-20 niche ideas using these five discovery frameworks:

## Framework 1: Skills × Market Gaps

For each of my top 3-5 skills:
1. Identify 3-5 market segments that need this skill
2. Look for underserved sub-niches within each segment
3. Validate each with evidence (subreddit size, search volume, competitor count)

Example output:
- Skill: Python automation
  - Niche 1: Marketing automation for solo entrepreneurs (r/solopreneur 250k members)
  - Niche 2: Spreadsheet automation for freelance accountants (Google Trends: 320 searches/month)
  - Niche 3: Web scraping for real estate investors (LinkedIn group: 15k members, growing)

## Framework 2: Interest × Pain Points

For each of my top 3-5 interests:
1. Identify 3-5 common pain points in this space
2. Find sub-communities with specific, acute problems
3. Check if existing solutions are overpriced, generic, or outdated

Example output:
- Interest: Personal finance
  - Niche 1: Budgeting for freelancers with irregular income (pain: irregular cash flow)
  - Niche 2: Retirement planning for gig economy workers (pain: no employer matching)
  - Niche 3: Tax optimization for remote workers (pain: state/federal complexity)

## Framework 3: Trend Surfing

Identify 5 emerging trends and find micro-niches within them:
1. Research Google Trends, Reddit growth, Twitter/LinkedIn buzz
2. Look for nascent communities (500-5,000 active members)
3. Validate trend isn't saturated yet (check existing competitor count)

Example output:
- Trend: AI tools adoption (Google Trends: +500% YoY)
  - Niche 1: AI for creative writers (fiction, screenplays) - 3 competitors
  - Niche 2: AI for game developers (asset generation) - 1-2 competitors
  - Niche 3: AI for educators (lesson planning) - 5-8 competitors

## Framework 4: Cross-Pollination

Combine two unrelated interests or skills to create novel niches:
1. Interest A × Interest B = Unexpected opportunity
2. Skill A × Industry B = Rare combination

Example output:
- Fitness × Remote work = "Ergonomic fitness for desk workers"
- Cooking × Data analysis = "Recipe optimization with nutrition science"
- Design × Sustainability = "Eco-friendly graphic design resources"

## Framework 5: Demographic Slicing

Take a broad market and slice by specific demographics:
1. Age group: Millennials, Gen Z, Gen X, Boomers
2. Life stage: New parents, recent grads, empty nesters, retirees
3. Profession: Teachers, nurses, engineers, consultants

Example output:
- Broad market: Language learning
  - Niche 1: Spanish for healthcare workers (9-5 scheduling, medical terminology)
  - Niche 2: Mandarin for business executives (networking, deal closure)
  - Niche 3: English for software engineers (technical writing, documentation)

- **Core problem:** What pain point does this address?
- **Discovery framework:** Which framework above generated this?
- **Quick validation:**
  - Community size: [Subreddit members, Discord active users, etc.]
  - Search volume estimate: [Google Trends level, search queries]
  - Existing competitors: [0-2 direct competitors, 3-5, 5+, very saturated]
  - Budget indicator: [Do they spend money on solutions? Yes/Maybe/Unclear]
- **Stream fit:** [AI Templates | Content Publishing | POD | Flexible] - Why this match?
- **Preliminary score:** [1-10 scale] - Your gut feeling on viability

---

## Validation Checklist

Before progressing to audience profiling, quickly validate each promising niche:

- [ ] **Audience exists and is reachable** - Can I find them online? (subreddit, forum, Discord, Slack, etc.)
- [ ] **Problem is specific** - Not too broad (e.g., "marketing") or vague
- [ ] **Willingness to pay** - Evidence they spend $20-500 on solutions (product reviews, spending discussions)
- [ ] **Competition not saturated** - Room for new entrants (less than 10 dominant competitors)
- [ ] **Fits your skills/interests** - You can credibly serve this niche or learn quickly
- [ ] **Market size sufficient** - At least 1,000+ people with this problem
```

### Tips for Best Results

1. **Generate 20 ideas, not 5** - Most people stop too early. The best niches emerge after idea #8-12. Push yourself to 20+.

2. **Use multiple frameworks** - Don't just use one. Combine 3-4 frameworks for diverse ideas. Each framework reveals different opportunities.

3. **Don't self-censor** - Include "weird" or "narrow" ideas. Sometimes the most specific micro-niches are the most profitable.

4. **Spend only 2-3 minutes validating each niche** - This is discovery, not deep research. Quick signals (subreddit size, search volume) are enough. Save detailed validation for top 5.

5. **Look for micro-niches** - "Fitness" is too broad. "Fitness for remote workers with desk jobs" is goldilocks: specific enough to win, broad enough to sustain.

6. **Check for budget** - Your target audience must have money and spend it on solutions. High passion ≠ high budget. Look for evidence of spending ($20-500 range for digital products).

7. **Verify communities exist** - You must be able to find and reach your audience. No community = no marketing channel. Check: Subreddits, Discord, Slack, LinkedIn groups, forums.

### Common Mistakes to Avoid

| Mistake | Why It Fails | Fix |
|---------|--------------|-----|
| Choosing niches you don't care about | You'll lose motivation after 2-3 months and abandon | Require at least 6/10 personal interest level |
| Going too broad ("marketing" or "fitness") | Competition is too intense; can't win | Drill down 2-3 levels: marketing → email → for B2B SaaS → for startups |
| Ignoring budget validation | Niche can't afford your product; scaling stalls at $500/month | Evidence required: Do they spend $20-200 on tools? Check: product reviews, testimonials, pricing discussions |
| Chasing trends too late | Niche is already saturated by time you launch | Look for trends <12 months old; check Google Trends for growth direction |
| Skipping community check | Can't reach your audience; marketing becomes impossible | Require at least one active community (1,000+ members); plan how you'll find them |
| Picking too many niches | Spread yourself too thin; none succeed | Limit to 3-5 finalists; commit to one deeply |

## Stream-Specific Guidance

### For AI Template Developers

Look for niches with:
- **Knowledge workers** who repeat similar tasks (lawyers, consultants, marketers, accountants)
- **Creators** who produce at scale (YouTubers, podcasters, newsletter authors, course creators)
- **Analysts** who need frameworks (data scientists, business analysts, researchers)
- **Professionals** with expensive hourly rates (engineers, doctors, architects) - high willingness to pay

Best frameworks: **Skills × Market Gaps**, **Interest × Pain Points**

### For Content Publishers

Look for niches with:
- **Passionate communities** with shared interests (hobbies, careers, life stages)
- **Underserved search intents** (questions with few good answers, outdated content)
- **Emerging technologies/trends** needing educational content (AI, Web3, new platforms)
- **Professional development** audiences (willing to pay for expertise, actively learning)

Best frameworks: **Trend Surfing**, **Demographic Slicing**, **Interest × Pain Points**

### For POD Designers

Look for niches with:
- **Community-driven identity** with inside jokes, catchphrases, visual styles (fandoms, professions, beliefs)
- **Lifestyle/identity** people want to display (career pride, hobby passion, life milestones)
- **Gift-giving occasions** (birthdays, holidays, milestones) for specific demographics
- **Strong visual culture** (designs are central to community identity, not afterthought)

Best frameworks: **Cross-Pollination**, **Demographic Slicing**, **Interest × Pain Points**
