# Print on Demand Sub-Skill

**Design and sell niche merchandise through print fulfillment partners with zero inventory risk.**

---

## Overview

The Print on Demand (POD) sub-skill generates passive income by designing merchandise (apparel, home goods, accessories) and uploading to fulfillment platforms that handle production, shipping, and returns. Perfect for technical audiences (developer/designer merchandise) and building community brand.

**Core Value Proposition:**
- Zero inventory cost (print only when ordered)
- Passive income (designs earn indefinitely)
- Brand building (strengthens community/identity)
- Minimal operational overhead
- Low barrier to entry

## Revenue Model

**Pricing strategy:**
- Redbubble markup: 20-50% above base cost (built-in audience)
- Printify markup: 50-100%+ (no built-in traffic)
- Volume play: High volume + modest margins vs. low volume + high margins

**Example margins:**
- T-shirt base cost: $5 → Sell $19.99 → Margin: $8-10 per sale
- Hoodie base cost: $12 → Sell $39.99 → Margin: $15-18 per sale
- Sticker base cost: $0.50 → Sell $3.99 → Margin: $2-3 per sale

**Target Year 1:** $1,000/month = ~100 items sold/month at $10 avg margin

## Platform Selection

### Redbubble (Recommended Start)
- **Best for:** Passive income, discovery
- **How it works:** Upload design, Redbubble handles everything
- **Audience:** 1B+ searches/month, built-in marketplace
- **Revenue:** 20-50% margin depending on markup
- **Effort:** Upload once, earn passively
- **Why:** Largest built-in audience, zero operational complexity

### Printful / Printify (Custom Brand)
- **Best for:** Branded merchandise, higher margins
- **How it works:** Integrate with own store (Shopify, WooCommerce)
- **Audience:** Your traffic only
- **Revenue:** 50-100%+ margin (depends on pricing)
- **Effort:** Drive your own traffic
- **When to move:** $1K+/month on Redbubble, ready for custom brand

## Design Quality Standards

**Visual:**
- ✅ High contrast (readable at thumbnail size)
- ✅ Simple layout (not cluttered)
- ✅ Professional typography (no Comic Sans!)
- ✅ Consistent branding if part of series

**Technical:**
- ✅ High resolution (300 DPI for print)
- ✅ Correct color space (CMYK for print)
- ✅ Mockups showing design on actual product

**Marketing:**
- ✅ Clear product preview (not just design)
- ✅ Compelling title (searchable keywords)
- ✅ Tag strategy (discoverable but not spammy)
- ✅ Description (why someone should buy)

## Workflow Overview

### 1. Niche & Design Research
**Duration:** 1-2 hours

1. Choose target audience (developers, designers, etc.)
2. Research existing designs (what's selling, what's not)
3. Identify underserved angles (unique twist on common theme)
4. Analyze top sellers (tags, pricing, descriptions)

**File:** `PROMPT-LIBRARY.md` - Style guide reference

### 2. Design Creation
**Duration:** 1-4 hours per design

1. Brainstorm concept (what would this audience want on apparel?)
2. Sketch or wireframe
3. Create final design (Figma, Adobe, Procreate)
4. Generate mockups (t-shirt on model, hoodie in scene, etc.)
5. Export for platform (check requirements)

**File:** `AGENT.md` - Use for ideation and direction

### 3. Upload & Optimize
**Duration:** 30 mins per design

1. Upload to chosen platform (Redbubble, Printful, etc.)
2. Craft compelling title (keywords for discoverability)
3. Write description (benefits, use case, humor)
4. Add tags (platform's hashtag system)
5. Set pricing (test different price points)

### 4. Performance Tracking
**Duration:** 15 mins weekly, 1 hour monthly

1. Track sales by product
2. Monitor returns/complaints
3. Identify bestsellers (focus here)
4. Remove underperformers after 3-6 months

**File:** `PROJECT-TRACKER.md`

## Design Inspiration & Execution

### Quick Formula for Developer Merch

1. **Take a common pain point** ("Debugging takes forever")
2. **Add technical humor** ("Debugging is 90% staring + 10% code")
3. **Make it visual** (Simple illustration + bold typography)
4. **Choose product** (Shirt for broad appeal, hoodie for commitment)

### Common Winning Formulas

| Formula | Example | Why It Works |
|---------|---------|---|
| **Quote + Niche Skill** | "I'm a designer" + design visual | Identity affirmation |
| **Problem + Punchline** | "Debugging" + absurd solution | Relatability + humor |
| **Inside Joke** | Localhost references, tech terminology | Signals membership |
| **Minimalist Tech** | Simple geometric + tech concept | Designer appeal |
| **Brutalist Humor** | Intentionally "bad" design (ironic) | Anti-establishment appeal |

## Tagging & Discoverability Strategy

**Redbubble tags (max 8):**
1. Core niche keyword ("developer", "designer", "data-scientist")
2. Specific technology ("python", "react", "figma")
3. Product type ("t-shirt", "hoodie", "mug")
4. Audience stage ("startup", "enterprise", "indie")
5. Humor/vibe ("funny", "minimalist", "vintage")
6. Use case ("gift", "office", "casual")
7-8. Long-tail keywords for uniqueness

**Example tags for "Debugging shirt":**
- developer
- debugging
- programming
- t-shirt
- software-engineer
- funny-shirt
- computer-humor
- tech-apparel

## Common Design Mistakes to Avoid

| Mistake | Why It Fails | Fix |
|---------|---|---|
| Tiny text on shirt | Unreadable at distance | Make text 3x larger |
| Too many colors | Expensive to print, cluttered | Stick to 2-3 colors |
| Unclear messaging | People don't get the joke | Add context, make obvious |
| No product mockups | Hard to visualize on actual shirt | Use platform mockup tools |
| Trendy designs | Dated in 6 months | Timeless concepts work longer |
| Spammy keywords | Flagged by algorithm | Use natural, relevant tags |
