## CORE.md (24KB) - Design Philosophy & Principles

### Design Identity & Role
- **Partner not tool** - AI as collaborative design partner, not design generator
- **Interpret intent** beyond literal requirements
- **Challenge assumptions** when they conflict with user outcomes
- **Own the outcome** - treat deliverables as shipping under your name

### Output Spectrum & Fidelity Levels
Cascading outputs from discovery to production:
- **ASCII Wireframes** (~30-60 tokens) - Rapid brainstorming
- **PlantUML Salt** - Structure validation
- **Wireweave DSL** - Renders to HTML previews
- **SVG Mockups** - Visual direction (grayscale to hi-fi)
- **p5.js Prototype** - Interaction validation
- **Next.js/Textual/Desktop** - Production implementation
- **Figma Spec** - External handoff

### First Principles

1. **Design Serves Outcomes, Not Aesthetics**
   - Every decision traces to user outcome or business goal
   - Beautiful design that fails to convert is failed design
   - Aesthetics matter as means, not ends

2. **Restraint Is The Default**
   - One typeface family (two weights max)
   - Two-three color tones (one accent max)
   - Whitespace is structural, not decorative
   - Every element must justify existence

3. **Convention Over Innovation (Usually)**
   - Users visit to accomplish tasks, not admire interface
   - Familiar patterns reduce friction
   - Innovation justified only when conventional fails

4. **Accessibility First**
   - Not an afterthought or compliance checkbox
   - Core to good design thinking
   - Integrated from first sketch onward

### Design Decision Framework
- Rapid vs. validated (match output fidelity to decision type)
- Trade-offs between speed and completeness
- When to break rules (brand requirements, research evidence, explicit brief requests)

## MARKETING.md (33KB) - Marketing Validation & Positioning

### Core Concept: Lean Validation
- Test market assumptions BEFORE building
- 5-week sprint structure for hypothesis testing
- Position product through positioning platform + positioning statement
- Signals vs. noise in market feedback

### Key Methodologies

#### 1. Persona Creation
- Who is the customer?
- What problem are they solving?
- Why can't existing solutions work?
- What would they pay?

#### 2. Positioning Strategy
- Identify target market
- Define competitive set
- Articulate unique value
- Test messaging and positioning

#### 3. Channel Selection & Optimization
- Where do customers spend time?
- How to reach them cost-effectively?
- Ad creative patterns for each channel
- Conversion optimization tactics

#### 4. Landing Page Architecture
- Structure for conversion testing
- Copy hierarchy and persuasion patterns
- Form design and field validation
- Trust signals and social proof

#### 5. 5-Week Validation Sprint Structure
- Week 1: Persona research and positioning
- Week 2: Messaging and copy testing
- Week 3: Channel research and audience targeting
- Week 4: Landing page build and ad setup
- Week 5: Launch, iterate, measure

### Signal Detection Framework
- What indicates real market demand vs. noise?
- How to weight different signals?
- Metrics that actually predict success
- Common false positives to avoid
