# Nihilism

Nihilism is a family of philosophical views arguing that life is meaningless, that moral values are baseless, or that knowledge is impossible.

## Definition, related terms, and etymology

The precise definition of nihilism is disputed, and many other definitions and types of nihilism have been proposed.

The word nihilism is a combination of the Latin term nihil, meaning 'nothing', and the suffix -ism.

## Ethics and value theory

Forms of nihilism belonging to the fields of ethics and value theory question the existence of values, morality, and the meaning of life.

### Existential nihilism

Existential nihilism asserts that life is meaningless. It is not limited to the idea that some people fail to find meaning in their lives but makes the broader claim that human existence lacks a higher purpose.

Diverse possible reactions to existential nihilism have been proposed. **Friedrich Nietzsche** sought to use the disruptive force of nihilism to re-interpret all established ideals and values.

### Moral nihilism

Moral nihilism, also known as ethical nihilism, is the meta-ethical view that nothing is morally right or wrong.

## Epistemology

In epistemology or the theory of knowledge, nihilism challenges knowledge and truth.

### Relativism

According to relativism, knowledge, truth, or meaning are relative to the perspectives of specific individuals or cultural contexts.

### Skepticism

Skeptical interpretations go further by denying the existence of knowledge or truth altogether.

## Metaphysics

Metaphysical forms of nihilism question the nature of existence itself.

### Metaphysical nihilism

Metaphysical nihilism, also called ontological nihilism or blob theory, is the view that there might have been nothing at all. It states that the world does not depend on any particular concrete object.

The subtraction argument proposes a procedure to support this view. It states that the world could still exist if a specific rock was removed.

Opponents of metaphysical nihilism assert that an empty world is impossible, meaning that something must exist.

### Mereological nihilism

Mereological or compositional nihilism is the view that complex or composite objects do not exist. Only simple objects, like elementary particles, are considered real.

### Cosmic nihilism

Cosmic or cosmological nihilism is the view that reality is unintelligible and lacks inherent meaning.

## Other forms

### Political nihilism

Political nihilism advocates the destruction of established political institutions.

## History

Nihilism is primarily associated with modernity, emerging in the 18th and 19th centuries.
